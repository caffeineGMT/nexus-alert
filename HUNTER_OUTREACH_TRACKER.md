## 🤝 Self-Hunt Strategy (Backup Plan)

**When to Self-Hunt**:
- No hunters respond by 3 days before launch
- Want more control over the narrative
- Prefer authentic maker story
- Many #1 products are self-hunted!

**Benefits of Self-Hunting**:
✅ Full control over timing and messaging
✅ More authentic "maker story"
✅ Direct connection with community
✅ No need to coordinate with hunter
✅ Can leverage personal network

**Self-Hunt Checklist**:
- [ ] Optimize Product Hunt profile (photo, bio, past launches/comments)
- [ ] Prepare first comment (founder story) for immediate posting
- [ ] Alert beta users and early supporters (don't ask for upvotes, just share the launch)
- [ ] Schedule social posts (Twitter, LinkedIn) for 8 AM PT
- [ ] Plan to be hyper-responsive to comments all day

## 📊 Hunter Response Rate Expectations

**Industry Benchmarks** (based on PH maker reports):
- **Response Rate**: 10-30% (expect 1-3 responses out of 10 DMs)
- **Conversion Rate**: 5-15% (1-2 hunters agree out of 10 contacted)
- **Timeline**: Most responses within 24-48 hours

**If No Hunters Respond**:
1. **Self-hunt** (very common, often successful)
2. **Delay launch** by 1 week and try different hunters
3. **Leverage existing network** (beta users, friends in startup community)

## 🎓 Best Practices

### DO:
✅ Research each hunter's past hunts (personalize your message)
✅ Be genuine and humble in your approach
✅ Offer early access (makes them feel special)
✅ Be flexible on timing (work with their schedule)
✅ Thank them regardless of outcome
✅ Keep communication professional but friendly

### DON'T:
❌ Mass DM hunters with generic message
❌ Be pushy or entitled
❌ Spam follow-ups (1 follow-up max)
❌ Reach out to Ryan Hoover first (he gets 100s of requests)
❌ Promise anything you can't deliver
❌ Ignore their suggestions/feedback
