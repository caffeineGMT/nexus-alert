Detailed Program Breakdown

Global Entry

Global Entry is the most popular Trusted Traveler Program, designed for frequent international air travelers returning to the United States.

What You Get:

  • Expedited customs screening when returning to the US by air
  • Use automated kiosks at major airports (skip the customs line)
  • TSA PreCheck included (expedited domestic security)
  • Valid at 130+ enrollment centers nationwide
  • Mobile Passport Control (MPC) access

Best For:

  • Frequent international business travelers
  • People who fly internationally 3+ times per year
  • Travelers who don't regularly cross US land borders
  • Anyone who wants the widest enrollment center availability

Limitations:

  • Does NOT include dedicated land border crossing lanes
  • Higher cost than NEXUS ($100 vs $50)
  • No benefits when entering Canada (only when returning to US)

NEXUS

NEXUS is a joint US-Canada program that provides expedited processing at air, land, and sea borders between the two countries. It's the best value among all Trusted Traveler Programs.

What You Get:

  • All Global Entry benefits (air, customs, TSA PreCheck)
  • Dedicated NEXUS lanes at US-Canada land border crossings (huge time savings)
  • Expedited processing when flying between US and Canada (both directions)
  • Marine NEXUS lanes for boat travelers
  • Half the cost of Global Entry ($50 vs $100)

Best For:

  • Anyone who crosses the US-Canada border regularly
  • International travelers who also visit Canada (you get benefits in both countries)
  • Budget-conscious travelers who want maximum value
  • People near US-Canada border cities (Detroit, Seattle, Buffalo)

Limitations:

  • Requires interview at a US-Canada border location (fewer enrollment centers)
  • Both US and Canadian customs must approve you (dual screening process)
  • Appointments can be harder to get due to limited locations

Pro Tip: If you cross the US-Canada border even just 2-3 times per year, NEXUS pays for itself immediately. The time savings at land borders alone are worth it—NEXUS lanes can save you 1-2 hours during peak crossing times.

SENTRI

SENTRI (Secure Electronic Network for Travelers Rapid Inspection) is designed for frequent travelers crossing the US-Mexico border.

What You Get:

  • All Global Entry benefits (air, customs, TSA PreCheck)
  • Dedicated SENTRI lanes at US-Mexico land border crossings (massive time savings)
  • Expedited processing at pedestrian crossings (Ready Lanes for walkers)
  • Vehicle registration for SENTRI lanes
  • Valid at all Global Entry kiosks nationwide

Best For:

  • Anyone who crosses the US-Mexico border regularly
  • Business travelers or residents in border cities (San Diego, El Paso, Laredo)
  • People who drive across the border frequently
  • Frequent visitors to Mexico who need fast re-entry to the US

Limitations:

  • Highest cost among all programs ($122.25)
  • Requires interview at a US-Mexico border location (limited to southwestern US)
  • Vehicle must be registered separately (additional process)
  • Does NOT provide benefits when entering Mexico (only returning to US)

Important: If you cross the US-Mexico border during peak times (mornings, weekends), SENTRI can save you 2-4 hours per crossing. Standard lanes can have 2-3 hour waits, while SENTRI lanes average 5-15 minutes.

Which Program Should You Choose?

The right program depends entirely on your travel patterns. Here's a simple decision tree:

Choose NEXUS if:

  • You cross the US-Canada border 2+ times per year (by any mode)
  • You travel internationally AND visit Canada occasionally
  • You want the best value (all benefits for half the cost)
  • You're willing to travel to a border location for your interview

NEXUS is the best deal for most people. Even if you only visit Canada once or twice a year, it includes everything Global Entry offers at half the price.

Choose Global Entry if:

  • You travel internationally frequently but rarely visit Canada or Mexico
  • You need a convenient enrollment center location (130+ options)
  • You can't easily get to a US-Canada border for NEXUS interview
  • You prioritize convenience over cost (NEXUS requires border interview)

Global Entry makes sense if you fly internationally often but never cross land borders, or if getting to a border enrollment center is impractical.

Choose SENTRI if:

  • You cross the US-Mexico border regularly (weekly or monthly)
  • You live or work near the US-Mexico border
  • You drive across the border frequently
  • Time savings at border crossings are critical for you

SENTRI is essential if you're a frequent US-Mexico border crosser. The time savings alone justify the higher cost if you cross even once per month.

Can You Have Multiple Programs?

Yes! You can hold multiple Trusted Traveler memberships simultaneously. In fact, many frequent travelers have both NEXUS and SENTRI if they cross both the Canadian and Mexican borders.

Common Combinations:

  • NEXUS + SENTRI: For people who cross both northern and southern borders
  • Global Entry + SENTRI: For international travelers who also frequently visit Mexico

Note: You don't need Global Entry if you have NEXUS or SENTRI—both include all Global Entry benefits. Only get multiple programs if you need the specific border lane access they provide.
