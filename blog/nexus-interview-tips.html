NEXUS Interview Tips: Everything You Need to Know to Pass

Your NEXUS interview is the final step before getting approved for 5 years of expedited travel. This complete guide covers exactly what to bring, what questions to expect, how to prepare, and the common mistakes that lead to denials. With proper preparation, you'll pass on the first try.

What to Expect at Your NEXUS Interview

The NEXUS interview is a joint interview with both US Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) officers. It typically lasts 15-30 minutes and takes place at one of 13 NEXUS enrollment centers along the US-Canada border.

Interview Format:

  • You'll be interviewed by both US and Canadian officers
  • Interviews are usually conducted back-to-back (not simultaneous)
  • Each officer takes about 10-15 minutes
  • Officers verify your documents and ask background questions
  • Fingerprints and photo are taken for the NEXUS card (if not already done)

The interview is straightforward if you're honest, prepared, and bring the correct documents. Most applicants pass on the first try. The approval rate is approximately 85-90% for applicants who arrive prepared.

Required Documents Checklist

Bring original documents only. Photocopies are NOT accepted. Missing any required document will result in rescheduling your interview.

Essential Documents (Everyone)

  • ✓
    Valid Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your interview date
  • ✓
    Proof of Citizenship: Birth certificate, naturalization certificate, or citizenship card
  • ✓
    Government-Issued ID: Driver's license or state ID (in addition to passport)
  • ✓
    Proof of Residency: Recent utility bill, lease agreement, or mortgage statement showing your current address
  • ✓
    GOES Conditional Approval Letter: Print this from your GOES account or bring it on your phone

Additional Documents (If Applicable)

  • •
    Green Card Holders: Bring your Permanent Resident Card (Green Card)
  • •
    Canadian Permanent Residents: Bring your PR card or Confirmation of Permanent Residence
  • •
    Name Change Documents: Marriage certificate, divorce decree, or legal name change documents if your current name differs from birth certificate
  • •
    Vehicle Registration (optional): If you plan to use NEXUS lanes with a vehicle, bring registration and proof of insurance
  • •
    Minors (under 18): Birth certificate, custody documents if applicable, and consent letter from non-accompanying parent

⚠️ Pro Tip: Create a checklist and verify you have every document the night before your interview. Place all documents in a folder so you can easily hand them over. Missing even one document means rescheduling (which can take months).

Common NEXUS Interview Questions

The questions are straightforward and designed to verify your identity, background, and eligibility. Answer honestly and directly. Here are the most common questions asked:

Personal Background

  • What is your current occupation?
  • Where do you work? What does your company do?
  • Have you ever been arrested or convicted of a crime?
  • Have you ever been denied entry to the US or Canada? If yes, why?
  • Do you have any pending legal issues or court cases?
  • Have you ever overstayed a visa or violated immigration laws in any country?

Travel History & Plans

  • Why are you applying for NEXUS?
  • How often do you cross the US-Canada border?
  • What is the purpose of your border crossings? (work, leisure, etc.)
  • Which countries have you visited in the past 5 years?
  • Do you have family or friends in Canada? Where?
  • Do you own property in Canada or the US?

Customs & Import Questions

  • Have you ever been stopped by customs? If yes, why?
  • Have you ever failed to declare goods when crossing a border?
  • What do you typically bring across the border?
  • Are you aware of the customs declaration requirements for both countries?

Verification Questions

  • Confirm your current address
  • Confirm your employment details
  • Verify information from your application (they'll reference specific details)
  • Explain any gaps in employment or unusual travel patterns

How to Answer Effectively:

  • Be honest. Officers can access your full travel and legal history. Lying or omitting information will result in immediate denial.
  • Keep answers concise. Don't over-explain or volunteer unnecessary information.
  • If you don't understand a question, ask for clarification. It's better to ask than to answer incorrectly.
  • Stay calm and professional. Officers are evaluating your demeanor and trustworthiness.
  • Admit past mistakes if asked. A minor infraction years ago (if disclosed in your application) won't necessarily disqualify you. Lying about it will.

Day-of-Interview Preparation

How you present yourself matters. Here's how to make the best impression:

✅ Arrive 15-30 Minutes Early

NEXUS enrollment centers are often in or near border crossings. Traffic can be unpredictable. Arriving late may result in missing your appointment and having to reschedule.

✅ Dress Professionally

You don't need a suit, but dress neatly (business casual). First impressions matter, and officers are evaluating your overall presentation.

✅ Bring All Documents in a Folder

Organize your documents in the order listed above. Being organized signals that you're prepared and take the process seriously.

✅ Review Your Application

Print and review your GOES application the night before. Officers will reference details from your application, and you need to remember what you wrote (especially employment history and travel dates).

✅ Turn Off Your Phone

No phones, cameras, or electronic devices are allowed in the interview area. Turn your phone off (not just silent) before entering.

✅ Leave Weapons and Prohibited Items in Your Car

Enrollment centers have strict security. Don't bring knives, pepper spray, or anything that could be considered a weapon. You'll go through a security screening.

Common Mistakes That Lead to Denial

Most denials are preventable. Avoid these critical mistakes:

  • ❌ Lying or omitting information on your application: Officers have access to your full criminal, travel, and customs history across multiple databases. If you lied on your application (even about something minor), it will be discovered and result in immediate denial.
  • ❌ Arriving without required documents: Missing your birth certificate, passport, or proof of residency means instant rescheduling. This can set you back months.
  • ❌ Being evasive or defensive: If an officer asks a follow-up question, they're not accusing you of anything—they're verifying information. Stay calm and answer directly.
  • ❌ Inconsistent answers between US and Canadian officers: You'll be asked similar questions by both officers. Make sure your answers are consistent. Contradictions raise red flags.
  • ❌ Past customs violations or undeclared goods: If you've been caught bringing undeclared goods across the border (alcohol, tobacco, goods over the duty-free limit), this can disqualify you. Be prepared to explain any past incidents.
  • ❌ DUI or criminal convictions not disclosed: A DUI, even from years ago, can disqualify you from NEXUS—especially if you didn't disclose it on your application. If you have a criminal record, consult with an immigration attorney before applying.
  • ❌ Bringing a friend or family member into the interview: Only the applicant is allowed in the interview room (exception: parents of minors). Don't bring anyone with you unless they're also interviewing.

What Happens After the Interview?

In most cases, you'll receive a decision immediately at the end of your interview.

✅ If Approved:

  • Your NEXUS card will be mailed to you within 7-10 business days
  • You can start using your NEXUS benefits immediately
  • Your membership is valid for 5 years from the approval date
  • Add your NEXUS number (also called PASSID) to airline profiles for TSA PreCheck

⏳ If Pending Additional Review:

Sometimes officers need to verify additional information before approving. You'll be notified via email or mail within 30 days.

❌ If Denied:

You'll receive a denial letter explaining the reason. Common reasons include:

  • Criminal convictions (especially DUI or customs violations)
  • False information on the application
  • Immigration violations or overstays
  • Outstanding warrants or legal issues

You can reapply after 1 year, but you must address the underlying issue first.

Tips for First-Time NEXUS Applicants

  • •
    Practice your answers: Review the common questions above and practice answering them out loud. You want to sound confident and natural, not rehearsed or nervous.
  • •
    Be prepared to explain your travel patterns: If you've traveled to high-risk countries or have unusual travel frequency, be ready to explain the purpose (work, tourism, family, etc.).
  • •
    Know your dates: Officers may ask specific dates about your employment, travel, or address history. Have this information fresh in your mind.
  • •
    Don't bring children unless they're also applying: The enrollment center is a secure area. Leave kids at home if they're not interviewing.
  • •
    Check border wait times: If your enrollment center is on the US side and you're crossing from Canada (or vice versa), check border wait times before you leave.

Final Checklist

The Night Before Your Interview:

  • ☐Print and review your GOES conditional approval letter
  • ☐Gather all required documents and place them in a folder
  • ☐Review your application answers from GOES
  • ☐Set an alarm to arrive 30 minutes early
  • ☐Check border wait times and plan your route
  • ☐Choose professional attire (business casual minimum)
  • ☐Practice answering common interview questions out loud

Final Thoughts

The NEXUS interview is straightforward if you're prepared. Bring the right documents, answer honestly, and present yourself professionally. Most applicants pass on the first try.

The key is preparation. Review your application, organize your documents, and practice your answers to common questions. Officers aren't trying to trick you—they're simply verifying that you're a low-risk traveler who can be trusted with expedited border crossing privileges.

Once approved, you'll enjoy 5 years of expedited travel through US and Canadian borders, TSA PreCheck access, and significantly shorter wait times at airports and border crossings. It's worth the effort.
