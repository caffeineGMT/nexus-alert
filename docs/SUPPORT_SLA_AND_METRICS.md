# Customer Support SLA & Metrics

**Service Level Agreement & Performance Tracking**

**Effective Date:** Chrome Web Store Launch
**Last Updated:** March 18, 2026

---

## 🎯 Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

### Response Time SLAs

| Tier | Channel | First Response | Resolution Target |
|------|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Free** | Email | < 48 hours | < 5 business days |
| **Free** | Live Chat | < 2 hours (business hours) | Same session or < 24 hours |
| **Premium** | Email | < 24 hours | < 3 business days |
| **Premium** | Live Chat | < 30 minutes (business hours) | Same session or < 12 hours |
| **Critical Bug** | Any | < 4 hours | < 48 hours |

**Business Hours:** 9 AM - 6 PM Pacific Time, Monday - Friday

**Holidays:** US Federal holidays excluded from business day calculations

---

### Issue Priority Levels

#### P0 - Critical (All Hands)
- Extension completely non-functional for all users
- Security vulnerability discovered
- Payment processing down (can't collect revenue)
- Chrome Web Store delisting threat

**SLA:** First response < 1 hour, resolution < 4 hours

---

#### P1 - High (Urgent)
- Extension not working for individual Premium user
- Premium features not activating after payment
- Widespread notification failures
- Critical bug affecting > 100 users

**SLA:** First response < 4 hours, resolution < 24 hours

---

#### P2 - Medium (Important)
- Individual user unable to receive notifications
- Installation issues
- Extension performance degradation
- Billing questions
- Refund requests

**SLA:**
- Free: First response < 48 hours
- Premium: First response < 24 hours

---

#### P3 - Low (Routine)
- General questions
- Feature requests
- Documentation feedback
- "How do I..." questions

**SLA:** First response < 72 hours (or direct to Help Center)

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### 1. Response Time Metrics

**Target:** 95% of tickets meet SLA

| Metric | Free Tier Target | Premium Tier Target |
|--------|------------------|---------------------|
| First Response Time (Email) | < 48h (95% of tickets) | < 24h (98% of tickets) |
| First Response Time (Chat) | < 2h (90% of chats) | < 30m (95% of chats) |
| Average Resolution Time | < 5 business days | < 3 business days |
| Same-Session Resolution (Chat) | > 60% | > 75% |

---

### 2. Quality Metrics

**Target:** > 90% customer satisfaction

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT) | > 4.5/5 | Post-chat/ticket surveys in Crisp |
| First Contact Resolution (FCR) | > 60% | % of issues resolved without follow-up |
| Ticket Reopening Rate | < 10% | % of tickets reopened by customer |
| Help Article Effectiveness | > 70% | % of users who find article helpful (thumbs up) |

---

### 3. Volume Metrics

**Expected Volume (First Month Post-Launch):**

| Channel | Daily Volume | Weekly Volume | Monthly Volume |
|---------|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| Email | 10-30 | 70-210 | 300-900 |
| Live Chat | 5-15 | 35-105 | 150-450 |
| Help Center Views | 50-200 | 350-1400 | 1500-6000 |

**Scaling Triggers:**
- If daily email > 50: Add second support operator
- If chat queue > 10: Add chat-only operator
- If response SLA miss rate > 10%: Expand team or hours

---

### 4. Efficiency Metrics

| Metric | Target | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Average Handle Time (Chat) | < 10 minutes | Measure efficiency without rushing |
| Average Email Length | 150-300 words | Concise but thorough |
| Canned Response Usage | 40-60% | Balance automation with personalization |
| Help Center Deflection Rate | > 30% | % who find answer without contacting support |

## 📋 Daily Support Checklist

**Morning (9 AM PT):**
- [ ] Check email inbox for overnight tickets
- [ ] Review Crisp chat queue
- [ ] Respond to any Premium user tickets first
- [ ] Check for critical bugs reported overnight
- [ ] Review yesterday's metrics (CSAT, response times)

**Midday (12 PM PT):**
- [ ] Follow up on pending tickets
- [ ] Update canned responses if needed
- [ ] Check help center search queries (identify gaps)
- [ ] Clear chat queue

**Afternoon (3 PM PT):**
- [ ] Respond to new tickets
- [ ] Escalate any unresolved complex issues
- [ ] Update internal documentation if new issue found
- [ ] Prepare handoff notes if multi-operator team

**End of Day (6 PM PT):**
- [ ] Ensure all high-priority tickets acknowledged
- [ ] Set status to "away" in Crisp
- [ ] Enable email autoresponder
- [ ] Document any ongoing issues for next day

## 🚨 Escalation SLAs

When escalating to dev team or management:

| Issue Type | Escalation Response SLA | Resolution SLA |
|------------|-------------------------|----------------|
| Critical Bug (P0) | Acknowledge < 1 hour | Fix deployed < 24 hours |
| High Priority Bug (P1) | Acknowledge < 4 hours | Fix deployed < 5 days |
| Medium Bug (P2) | Acknowledge < 24 hours | Fix in next release |
| Feature Request | Acknowledge < 1 week | Roadmap decision < 1 month |

**Escalation Format:**
```
**Issue:** [Brief description]
**Priority:** P0/P1/P2/P3
**Affected Users:** [Number or "widespread"]
**Steps to Reproduce:**
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]

**Expected Behavior:** [What should happen]
**Actual Behavior:** [What's happening]
**Browser/Version:** [Chrome 120, etc.]
**Extension Version:** [1.2.3]
**Workaround:** [If any]
**Customer Impact:** [How this affects users]
**Ticket IDs:** [Crisp/email ticket references]
```

## 💡 Quality Assurance Checks

### Every Support Response Should:

- [ ] **Be Proofread:** No typos or grammar errors
- [ ] **Be Personalized:** Use customer's name, reference their specific issue
- [ ] **Be Clear:** Step-by-step instructions, no jargon
- [ ] **Provide Value:** Link to help article, offer preventive tips
- [ ] **Set Expectations:** "I'll follow up within 24 hours..."
- [ ] **Be Empathetic:** Acknowledge frustration, apologize for issues
- [ ] **End with Action:** "Let me know if this helps!" or "Questions?"

### Weekly QA Spot Checks:

Support lead randomly reviews 5 tickets/week:
- Response quality
- SLA compliance
- Tone and professionalism
- Accuracy of information
- Use of help articles

**Feedback delivered within 48 hours of review.**
