## Ad Copy Writing Rules

### Google Ads Copy Guidelines

1. **Always include benefit in Headline 1**: "Never Miss", "Get Instant Alerts", "Book Weeks Sooner"
2. **Social proof in Headline 2 or 3**: "500+ Users", "Trusted by Travelers", "Proven Results"
3. **Call to action in description**: "Install Free", "Get Started", "Add to Chrome"
4. **Use numbers**: "24/7", "100+ locations", "30 seconds to install"
5. **Address pain point**: "Stop refreshing", "No manual checking", "Never miss again"
6. **Keyword in headline**: Include exact match keyword in at least one headline
7. **Mobile-first**: Keep headlines under 30 chars for mobile display

### Facebook Ads Copy Guidelines

1. **Hook in first sentence**: Ask question or state shocking fact
2. **Keep it scannable**: Short sentences, bullet points, emojis sparingly
3. **Social proof early**: "500+ users" in first 2 lines
4. **One clear CTA**: "Install Free", "Learn More", or "Get Started" - not all three
5. **Urgency when relevant**: "Slots disappear in minutes", "Don't wait 6 months"
6. **Benefit-focused**: Lead with "what's in it for them", not features
7. **Test variations**: A/B test question vs. statement, short vs. long copy

## A/B Testing Strategy

### Week 1-2: Test Headlines
- Version A: Feature-focused ("Never Miss a Slot")
- Version B: Pain point-focused ("Tired of Refreshing?")
- Winner: Keep version with higher CTR

### Week 3-4: Test Descriptions
- Version A: Feature list
- Version B: Benefit statement
- Winner: Keep version with higher conversion rate

### Week 5+: Test Creatives (Facebook)
- Carousel vs. Single Image
- Testimonial vs. Product screenshot
- Short copy vs. Long copy
- Winner: Scale budget to winner

### Winning Criteria
- **Google Ads**: CTR > 5% and Quality Score ≥ 7
- **Facebook Ads**: CTR > 2% and CPM < $15
- Run for minimum 500 impressions before deciding winner

## Brand Voice & Tone

**Voice**: Direct, helpful, confident
**Tone**: Friendly but not overly casual, informative but not technical

**Do**:
- Use "you" language (not "users" or "customers")
- Be specific with numbers ("500+ users", "24/7 monitoring")
- Focus on benefits over features
- Address pain points directly
- Use active voice ("Get instant alerts" not "Alerts are provided")

**Don't**:
- Use jargon ("daemon", "API", "polling")
- Make unrealistic promises ("guaranteed appointment")
- Use ALL CAPS excessively
- Over-emoji (max 1-2 per ad)
- Use superlatives without proof ("best", "fastest")

## Legal & Compliance

**Disclaimers** (add to landing pages):
- "NEXUS Alert is not affiliated with CBP, DHS, or GOES"
- "Results may vary. We cannot guarantee appointment availability"
- "Premium pricing subject to change"

**Trademark Guidelines**:
- ✅ Can use: "NEXUS", "Global Entry", "SENTRI" as descriptive terms
- ❌ Cannot use: CBP logo, DHS logo, official government badges
- ✅ Can say: "Works with NEXUS, Global Entry & SENTRI"
- ❌ Cannot say: "Official NEXUS tool" or "Endorsed by CBP"

**Facebook Ad Policies**:
- No "clickbait" headlines
- No "before/after" health/weight claims (not applicable here)
- Must have clear CTA
- Landing page must match ad promise

**Google Ads Policies**:
- Cannot use "coronavirus" or "COVID-19" keywords
- Cannot make guarantees about government appointments
- Must have clear pricing on landing page
- No trademark violations

## Performance Benchmarks

### Google Search Ads (Expected)
- **CTR**: 3-5% (industry average: 2%)
- **Quality Score**: 7-9/10
- **Conversion Rate**: 10-15% (landing page → install click)
- **CPC**: $0.40-0.80 (varies by keyword)

### Facebook Retargeting (Expected)
- **CTR**: 1.5-3% (retargeting typically higher than cold)
- **CPM**: $10-20 (USA/Canada)
- **Conversion Rate**: 15-25% (warm audience)
- **CPC**: $0.50-1.50

### If Metrics Below Benchmark
- CTR < 2%: Test new ad copy, improve headlines
- Quality Score < 6: Improve landing page relevance, add keywords to page
- Conversion < 8%: Simplify CTA, reduce friction, improve value prop
- CPC > $1: Lower bids, improve Quality Score, target long-tail keywords
