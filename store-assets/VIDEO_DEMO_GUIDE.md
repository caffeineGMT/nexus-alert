## 🎥 Recording Setup

### Equipment Needed:
- **Screen recorder:** Loom, OBS Studio, ScreenFlow, or QuickTime (macOS)
- **Microphone:** USB mic (Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica AT2020) or good headset
- **Video editor:** Final Cut Pro, Adobe Premiere, DaVinci Resolve, or iMovie

### Pre-Recording Checklist:
- [ ] Close all unnecessary apps and browser tabs
- [ ] Hide desktop icons (macOS: defaults write com.apple.finder CreateDesktop false)
- [ ] Clean browser (remove bookmarks bar, extensions except NEXUS Alert)
- [ ] Set browser to full screen or consistent window size
- [ ] Prepare all tabs in advance (GOES website, Chrome Web Store, booking page)
- [ ] Test microphone levels (voiceover should be -6dB to -3dB)
- [ ] Test screen recording quality (1080p minimum)
- [ ] Rehearse script 2-3 times for smooth delivery

### Recording Settings:
- **Resolution:** 1920×1080 (1080p)
- **Frame rate:** 30fps (60fps if showing animations)
- **Audio:** 48kHz, stereo, AAC codec
- **Bitrate:** 5-10 Mbps (high quality for YouTube)
- **Format:** MP4 (H.264 codec for best compatibility)

## 🎙️ Voiceover Recording Tips

### Voice Guidelines:
- **Tone:** Friendly, confident, upbeat (not salesy or robotic)
- **Pace:** Medium speed (not rushed, but not slow)
- **Emphasis:** Highlight key words ("24/7", "instant", "free")
- **Energy:** Smile while recording (it affects voice tone)
- **Clarity:** Enunciate clearly, avoid mumbling

### Recording Process:
1. **Warm up:** Hum, lip trills, tongue twisters (5 minutes)
2. **Record in quiet room:** Close windows, turn off AC/fan
3. **Use pop filter:** Reduce plosives (P, B, T sounds)
4. **Stand or sit upright:** Better breath support
5. **Record multiple takes:** Choose best sections in editing
6. **Leave pauses:** 1-2 seconds between sentences (easier to edit)

### Post-Processing:
- **Noise reduction:** Remove background hiss (Audacity, Adobe Audition)
- **EQ:** Boost low-mids (200-400Hz) for warmth, cut low rumble (<80Hz)
- **Compression:** Even out volume (ratio 3:1, threshold -18dB)
- **Normalization:** Peak at -3dB (leave headroom for music)
- **De-essing:** Reduce harsh "S" sounds (if needed)

## ✂️ Video Editing Workflow

### Step 1: Import & Organize
1. Import screen recordings, voiceover, music
2. Create bins/folders: "Video Clips", "Audio", "Music", "Graphics"
3. Label clips clearly (e.g., "01-GOES-website", "02-Install")

### Step 2: Rough Cut
1. Lay voiceover on timeline first (anchor track)
2. Add screen recordings to match voiceover timing
3. Cut for pacing (remove dead air, slow moments)
4. Ensure total length is 60 seconds (±2 seconds)

### Step 3: Add Captions
**Caption Specifications:**
- **Font:** Inter Bold or Montserrat Bold
- **Size:** 32-40px (readable on mobile)
- **Color:** White with black outline or shadow
- **Position:** Bottom third of screen (safe zone)
- **Duration:** Match voiceover timing
- **Style:** All caps for emphasis, sentence case for long text

**Caption Tool Options:**
- **Manual:** Add text layers in video editor
- **Auto-generated:** Rev.com, Descript, or YouTube auto-captions (edit for accuracy)
- **Hardcode:** Burn captions into video (recommended for PH)

**Sample Captions:**
```
[0:00-0:05] "Wait 6 months for a NEXUS appointment?"
[0:05-0:10] "Or refresh manually hoping to get lucky?"
[0:10-0:15] "NEXUS Alert monitors 24/7 for you"
[0:15-0:25] "Install free in 10 seconds"
[0:25-0:35] "Pick your locations and preferences"
[0:35-0:45] "Get INSTANT alerts when slots appear"
[0:45-0:50] "One click takes you straight to booking"
[0:50-0:55] "Upgrade for faster checks + email/SMS"
[0:55-0:60] "Use code PRODUCTHUNT for 1st month free"
```

### Step 4: Add Music
1. Import royalty-free background music
2. Place on separate audio track
3. Fade in at 0:00, fade out at 0:58
4. Mix at 40-60% volume (voiceover should dominate)
5. Duck music during voiceover (auto-ducking or manual keyframes)

**Music Sources (Royalty-Free):**
- **Epidemic Sound** (subscription, high quality)
- **Artlist** (subscription, professional grade)
- **Uppbeat** (free with attribution, good selection)
- **Bensound** (free, limited selection)
- **YouTube Audio Library** (free, hit-or-miss quality)

**Music Style:**
- Genre: Electronic, indie pop, upbeat corporate
- Mood: Optimistic, energetic, modern
- Tempo: 120-130 BPM
- Avoid: Lyrics (distracting), repetitive loops, cheesy stock music

### Step 5: Color Correction
1. Match color across clips (white balance, exposure)
2. Slight saturation boost (+10-15%) for vibrancy
3. Ensure brand colors are accurate (#3b82f6 blue, #22c55e green)
4. Export color space: sRGB (web standard)

### Step 6: Add Transitions
- **Use sparingly:** Mostly hard cuts, occasional fade
- **Timing:** 0.5-1 second max for fades
- **Avoid:** Cheesy wipes, spins, or dated effects
- **Crossfades:** Between sections (problem → solution)

### Step 7: Final Polish
1. Add sound effects (notification chime, click sounds)
2. Animate text overlays (subtle slide-in, no distracting motion)
3. Add logo watermark (bottom-right corner, 10% opacity)
4. End screen with CTA (5 seconds, clear and bold)

### Step 8: Export
**Export Settings:**
- Format: MP4 (H.264)
- Resolution: 1920×1080
- Frame rate: 30fps
- Bitrate: 8-10 Mbps (high quality)
- Audio: AAC, 192kbps, stereo
- Color space: sRGB

**Quality Check Before Export:**
- [ ] Total length: 60 seconds (±2 sec acceptable)
- [ ] Audio levels balanced (voiceover clear, music in background)
- [ ] Captions visible and accurate
- [ ] No black frames or stuttering
- [ ] Brand colors accurate
- [ ] CTA clear at end
- [ ] File size: 50-100MB (YouTube-friendly)

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

**Issue: Video looks pixelated on YouTube**
- Solution: Export at higher bitrate (10-15 Mbps) or 4K (3840×2160) then let YouTube downscale

**Issue: Audio is out of sync**
- Solution: Check frame rate consistency (all clips should be 30fps or 60fps, not mixed)

**Issue: Captions are hard to read**
- Solution: Increase font size, add black outline or drop shadow, use high-contrast colors

**Issue: Music drowns out voiceover**
- Solution: Lower music volume to 40-50%, apply ducking (auto-lower music when voiceover plays)

**Issue: Video is too long (>60 seconds)**
- Solution: Trim pauses, speed up sections slightly (1.1-1.2x), cut less critical moments

**Issue: YouTube embed doesn't work on PH**
- Solution: Use unlisted visibility (not private), ensure embeds are allowed in YouTube settings

**Issue: File size is too large (>100MB)**
- Solution: Lower bitrate to 5-8 Mbps, compress with Handbrake, or export at 720p (acceptable for web)

## 🚀 Final Tips

1. **Practice makes perfect:** Record 2-3 takes and choose the best one
2. **Show, don't tell:** Let the product speak for itself with clear visuals
3. **Keep it simple:** Don't overload with effects or information
4. **Test on mobile:** 50%+ of viewers watch on phones
5. **Optimize for mute:** Captions should convey full message (many watch muted)
6. **Hook in 3 seconds:** First frame must grab attention
7. **End with clarity:** CTA should be unmistakable ("Install free", "Use code PRODUCTHUNT")
8. **Get feedback:** Show to 2-3 people before publishing
9. **Update if needed:** After PH launch, consider updated version with testimonials
10. **Repurpose content:** Use clips for social media (Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok)

**Remember:** The video is selling the value, not just demonstrating features. Show the problem, present the solution, make it easy to act.
