### [0:20-1:30] WHAT ARE THESE PROGRAMS? (Talking Head + Graphics)

"First, let's define what we're comparing."

**Global Entry:**
"Global Entry is a US Customs and Border Protection program that gets you expedited clearance when entering the United States from international destinations. When you fly into the US from abroad, you skip the regular customs line and use a kiosk instead."

**[Graphic: Global Entry logo + "Expedited US entry via kiosk"]**

"It costs $100 for 5 years. That's $20/year. You also automatically get TSA PreCheck included, which is worth $78 if you bought it separately."

**NEXUS:**
"NEXUS is a joint program between the US and Canada. It gives you expedited processing when crossing the US-Canada border in BOTH directions — by land, air, or sea."

**[Graphic: NEXUS logo + "US-Canada border crossings (land, air, sea)"]**

"It costs $50 for 5 years. That's $10/year. Half the price of Global Entry. And here's the kicker: NEXUS includes Global Entry AND TSA PreCheck automatically."

---

### [1:30-3:00] FEATURE COMPARISON (Screen + Graphics)

"Let me break down the features side-by-side."

**[On-screen table comparison:]**

| Feature | NEXUS | Global Entry |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| **Cost** | $50 (5 years) | $100 (5 years) |
| **US Entry** | ✅ Expedited (kiosk) | ✅ Expedited (kiosk) |
| **Canada Entry** | ✅ Expedited (NEXUS lane) | ❌ No benefit |
| **TSA PreCheck** | ✅ Included | ✅ Included |

**[Talking head]**

"So if you just look at the features, NEXUS gives you everything Global Entry does, PLUS Canada entry benefits, PLUS land border crossing, for HALF the price."

"So why doesn't everyone just get NEXUS?"

---

### [3:00-4:30] THE CATCH: INTERVIEW LOCATION (Talking Head + Map)

"Here's the catch. NEXUS requires an in-person interview at a NEXUS enrollment center. And there are only 13 of them in the entire US — all along the Canadian border."

**[Map graphic: Show NEXUS enrollment centers highlighted - Blaine WA, Seattle, Buffalo, Detroit, etc.]**

"If you live in Seattle, Vancouver, Buffalo, or Detroit, great. You're close to a NEXUS center. If you live in Texas, Florida, California, or anywhere more than a few hours from the Canadian border, you have to travel to one of these centers for your interview."

"Global Entry, on the other hand, has over 100 enrollment centers across the US. Airports in every major city. Much more accessible."

**[Map graphic: Show Global Entry centers - hundreds of dots across US]**

"So the question becomes: Are the extra benefits of NEXUS worth the travel to a Canadian border enrollment center?"

---

### [4:30-6:00] WHO SHOULD GET NEXUS (Talking Head)

"Here's my recommendation. Get NEXUS if:"

**1. You Live Near the US-Canada Border**
"If you're in Seattle, Vancouver, Buffalo, Detroit, or within a few hours of the border, NEXUS is a no-brainer. Same benefits as Global Entry, half the price, plus you can use NEXUS lanes at land crossings."

**2. You Cross the Border Regularly**
"If you drive across the border even 2-3 times a year, the NEXUS lane saves you 20-40 minutes every trip. That adds up fast."

**3. You Travel Internationally AND to Canada**
"NEXUS gives you expedited entry into BOTH the US and Canada. Global Entry only helps with US entry."

**4. You Want to Save Money**
"$50 vs $100. If the nearest NEXUS center is only a 2-3 hour drive, the $50 savings pays for your gas."

---

### [6:00-7:00] WHO SHOULD GET GLOBAL ENTRY (Talking Head)

"Get Global Entry if:"

**1. You Don't Live Near the Canadian Border**
"If you're in Los Angeles, Miami, Houston, or any city far from Canada, traveling to a NEXUS center doesn't make sense. Just get Global Entry at your local airport."

**2. You Never Go to Canada**
"If you only travel internationally to Europe, Asia, Mexico, etc. — but never Canada — you don't need the Canada entry benefits. Global Entry is fine."

**3. You Want Faster Appointment Access**
"Global Entry has 100+ enrollment centers, so appointment availability is usually better. NEXUS centers are fewer and often have longer wait times."
