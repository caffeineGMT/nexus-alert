### [0:15-1:00] WHAT IS NEXUS ALERT? (Talking Head)

"NEXUS Alert is a free Chrome extension that monitors the CBP Trusted Traveler Programs website and notifies you the instant a NEXUS, Global Entry, or SENTRI appointment opens up."

**[Screen: Show Chrome Web Store page for NEXUS Alert]**

"It works for all three programs:"
- NEXUS (US-Canada border crossings)
- Global Entry (international air travel)
- SENTRI (US-Mexico border crossings)

"Instead of manually refreshing the website every day, the extension does it for you automatically. When a slot opens, you get a notification. Click it, book the appointment, done."

---

### [1:00-2:00] INSTALLATION (Screen Recording)

"Let's install it. Super simple."

**Step 1: Open Chrome Web Store**

"Open a new tab and go to chrome.google.com/webstore. In the search bar, type 'NEXUS Alert'."

**[Screen: Type in search bar, show NEXUS Alert extension in results]**

**Step 2: Add to Chrome**

"Click the blue 'Add to Chrome' button. Chrome will ask you to confirm the permissions. Click 'Add Extension'."

**[Screen: Click "Add to Chrome", show permissions dialog, click "Add Extension"]**

**Step 3: Pin the Extension**

"The extension icon appears in your toolbar. If you don't see it, click the puzzle piece icon and pin NEXUS Alert so it's always visible."

**[Screen: Click puzzle icon, find NEXUS Alert, click pin]**

"Done. Installation took 15 seconds."

---

### [2:00-3:30] INITIAL SETUP (Screen Recording)

"Now let's configure it."

**Step 1: Open the Extension Popup**

"Click the NEXUS Alert icon in your toolbar. This opens the main popup."

**[Screen: Click icon, show popup UI]**

**Step 2: Select Your Program**

"At the top, you'll see three tabs: NEXUS, Global Entry, and SENTRI. Click the one you're applying for."

**[Screen: Click NEXUS tab]**

**Step 3: Choose Your Enrollment Center**

"In the dropdown, you'll see all available enrollment centers. Pick the one closest to you. If you're flexible, you can monitor multiple locations — I recommend it."

**[Screen: Open dropdown, scroll through locations, select 'Blaine, WA']**

"I'm selecting Blaine, Washington because it's the closest to me in Vancouver. But I'm also going to add Seattle and Vancouver Airport as backups."

**[Screen: Click "Add Another Location", select Seattle, then Vancouver]**

**Step 4: Set Check Interval**

"This controls how often the extension checks for appointments. Free users can check every 30 minutes. Premium users can check as often as every 2 minutes."

**[Screen: Show interval slider, set to 10 minutes (premium)]**

"I'm setting mine to 10 minutes. That's a good balance between catching slots quickly and not hammering the CBP servers."

---

### [3:30-4:30] NOTIFICATION SETTINGS (Screen Recording)

"Let's make sure notifications are set up correctly."

**Step 1: Enable Desktop Notifications**

"Check the box that says 'Show desktop notifications'. This is critical — you want to know immediately when a slot opens."

**[Screen: Check notification checkbox]**

**Step 2: Enable Sound**

"Check 'Play sound on alert'. Trust me, you don't want to miss a notification because you were away from your desk."

**[Screen: Check sound checkbox, click "Test Notification" button]**

**[Screen: Notification appears with sound]**

**Step 3: Email Alerts (Premium Only)**

"If you upgrade to premium, you can also enable email alerts. This means you'll get notified even if your browser is closed."

**[Screen: Show email input field, enter email, check "Email alerts" box]**

"Enter your email and click 'Subscribe'. The extension will send you an email whenever an appointment opens."
